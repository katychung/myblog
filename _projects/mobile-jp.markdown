<!-- Context -->
<div class="full-width-section" style="background-color: #fff; padding: 4rem 0;">
  <div class="container">
    <h3 class="page-label mb-3">Context</h3>
    <p>One of Japan's largest entertainment companies — with a portfolio spanning dozens of internationally recognised franchises — wanted a new official consumer app to bring together their physical retail experience. The brief: a single place for fans to manage loyalty points, discover upcoming events and new products, and participate in store-based gamification tied to real-world visits.</p>
    <p>This was an agency project, contracted through a Tokyo design agency. The brief was to design and prototype the core app experience for iOS and Android, ready for launch.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- Challenge -->
<div class="full-width-section" style="background-color: #EDEFF2; padding: 4rem 0;">
  <div class="container">
    <h3 class="page-label mb-3">The Challenge</h3>
    <p>The app needed to do several things at once: act as a digital loyalty card and points wallet, surface relevant news and events across the company's brands, and layer in gamification tied to physical store visits — all within a single, coherent mobile experience.</p>
    <p>The added constraint was brand. The client has strict guidelines around how their IP — characters, logos, visual identity — can be used across surfaces. Every screen had to work within those guardrails while still feeling like a product that users would actually want to open. Getting that balance right on both iOS and Android, across a product with multiple distinct feature areas, was the core design challenge.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- Approach -->
<div class="full-width-section" style="background-color: #fff; padding: 4rem 0;">
  <div class="container">
    <h3 class="page-label mb-3">Approach</h3>
    <p>I worked alongside another UX designer across the full design process — from initial wireframes through to high-fidelity screens and detailed prototypes for handoff to the third-party development team.</p>
    <p>Early on, the brief came in without a clearly defined target user or product strategy. Before moving into wireframes, I pushed for that foundation to be established — working with the team to clarify personas and align on what the product was actually optimising for. That groundwork shaped the design decisions that followed.</p>
    <p>The prototypes at handoff were built to spec — detailed enough that the dev team had what they needed to build without ambiguity.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- Outcome -->
<div class="full-width-section" style="background-color: #EDEFF2; padding: 4rem 0;">
  <div class="container">
    <h3 class="page-label mb-3">Outcome</h3>
    <p>The app launched on the App Store and Google Play in December 2022, combining a digital loyalty card, personalised discovery feed, gamified store experiences, and a points system redeemable across the client's official retail network.</p>
    <p>The app has since reached 50,000+ installs on Google Play.</p>
